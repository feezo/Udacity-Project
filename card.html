Peacock

The collective term for these birds is peafowl . The males are peacocks and the females are peahens.
The babies are called peachicks.

  • Scientific name: Pavo cristatus
  • Lifespan : 10 – 25 years (In the wild)
  • Mass: 4 – 6 kg (Adult)
  • Higher classification: Pavo

Also known as a Peafowl, a peacock is a brightly colored, medium-sized bird that belongs to the Pheasant family class.
This bird is typically native to Asia, except for the Congo Peacock that lives in Africa. The Peacock is the national bird of India.
